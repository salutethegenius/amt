Concept
Three descending bars read as infrastructure layers — the visual shorthand for "we build the layer underneath." The tapering communicates depth, not decoration.
Strength
Highly scalable. Works at favicon size, on a business card, and on a billboard. The wordmark split (Kemis in navy / Digital in accent) lets each word carry weight independently.
Fits when
You want something that feels grounded and architectural — authority without aggression. The right choice if KemisDigital is talking to enterprise and government alongside SMEs.

Concept
The K is reimagined as a network diagram. Vertical stroke is the spine. Diagonals become connection lines. Circles are nodes — the vocabulary of data flow and ecosystem connectivity.
Strength
The accent dot at the top-right is the signature. It's memorable and unique — no other Bahamian tech company has this kind of mark. Communicates "digital ecosystem" instantly at a subconscious level.
Fits when
You want the logo to function as a conversation starter. The Node concept is best for KemisDigital pitching to SMEs who need to see that you're a different kind of company — not just another dev shop.

Concept
The KD monogram sits inside a square container with a precision-cut corner in accent color. The cut corner signals forward momentum and exactness — the mark of a builder, not a consultant.
Strength
Most symbol-forward of the three. Works best as an app icon, badge, or social media avatar. The container makes it immediately distinguishable at small sizes. The accent corner inversion on dark backgrounds adds versatility.
Fits when
KemisDigital plans to ship a product with its own branded app or interface — the mark becomes the app icon. Best choice if you want the brand to stand fully independent of the wordmark.
